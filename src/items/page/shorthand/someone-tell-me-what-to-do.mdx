You may notice that some letters (S, L, E, A) look similar. There are two reasons for this:

- This is a bold, stylized design: in this writing system, S and L are written as horizontal lines, but here I made these lines so short and thick that they become rectangles that are taller than they are wide. Meanwhile, E and A are written as dots (or optionally as horizontal lines), which I also drew as rectangles here.
- This writing system relies on context to distinguish between most letters. While S and L are both horizontal lines, the left end of L must be below the end of the previous consonant, while the left end of S must be connected to the previous consonant (if there is one). Meanwhile E and A, as vowels, are written above or below the preceding consonants, and in a normal style do not touch them at all.

[This page](/work/shorthand) explains the system in full.
